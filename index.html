Purpose

Accessing online materials in an era of high technological advancements is a fundamental right that everybody, regardless of their demographic and/ or disabilities, should have.

  • Learn more

Context

With the rise in cyber crime within the last two decades, CAPTCHA was created as a defensive tool to prevent malicious bots from accessing online material. It is now a technology that has been implemented on a widespread scale, being used on one-third of the top 100,000 websites in the world.

  • Learn more

Barriers

CAPTCHA contains accessability options for those who have physical impairments, however, these options are inadequate. Unfortunately, the interactive nature of CAPTCHA's tasks eliminates a significant amount of individuals with impairments, resulting in an unacceptable denial of service for these users.

  • Learn more

The Solution

What is it?

Version 3.0 is a prototype solution to the current state of CAPTCHA. Created by Safa Kubti, this technology provides a solution for the accessibility concerns surronding modern day CAPTCHA challenges.

What is different about it?

Version 3.0 integrates artificial intelligence, specifically facial recognition software, into CAPTCHA's interface in order to avoid the use of any motor, cognitive, visual, and/ or auditory task.

Why artificial intelligence?

Facial recognition software is already implemeneted and deployed in most modern smartphones. The use of facial recognition has become a common element found in a variety of applications.

How does it work?

Similar to how Apple's iPhones use facial recognition on their platform, instead of working through a challenge, users simply use their face to get access to an online platform via CAPTCHA.

What does accessibility mean?

Accessible design refers to creating an interface that is friendly and compatible with all users, regardless of their disability. Disabilities may include motor, cognitive, visual, and/ or autitory impairments.

Why is this important?

Certain types of CAPTCHAs frequently limit disabled individuals' ability to perform necessary operations that grant them access to online platforms. It is a fundamental right to have access to such materials in order to have fair and equal opporunities for all.
